# Asthma

## Overview
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways characterized by recurrent episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing. The airways become swollen and narrow, and may produce extra mucus.

## Types
- **Allergic Asthma**: Triggered by allergens (pollen, dust mites, pet dander)
- **Non-allergic Asthma**: Triggered by exercise, cold air, stress, infections
- **Occupational Asthma**: Caused by workplace irritants
- **Exercise-induced Bronchoconstriction**: Triggered specifically by physical activity

## Symptoms
- Wheezing (whistling sound when breathing)
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness or pain
- Coughing (especially at night or early morning)
- Difficulty sleeping due to breathing problems

## Triggers
- Airborne allergens (pollen, mold, dust mites, pet dander)
- Respiratory infections (colds, flu)
- Physical activity
- Cold air
- Air pollutants and irritants (smoke, chemical fumes)
- Certain medications (aspirin, NSAIDs, beta-blockers)
- Strong emotions and stress
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)

## Diagnosis
- Spirometry: Measures how much air you can exhale and how quickly
- Peak flow measurement
- Methacholine challenge test
- Allergy testing
- Chest X-ray to rule out other conditions

## Treatment
- **Quick-relief (rescue) medications**: Short-acting beta-agonists (albuterol/salbutamol) for immediate symptom relief
- **Long-term control medications**: Inhaled corticosteroids (fluticasone, budesonide) — most effective; long-acting beta-agonists (salmeterol); leukotriene modifiers (montelukast)
- **Biologics**: For severe asthma (omalizumab, mepolizumab)
- **Asthma action plan**: Written plan to manage symptoms and attacks

## Complications
- Permanent narrowing of airways (airway remodeling)
- Sleep disturbances
- Missed school/work days
- Severe asthma attacks requiring hospitalization
- Side effects from long-term medication use
