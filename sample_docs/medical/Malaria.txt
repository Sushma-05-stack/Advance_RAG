# Malaria

## Overview
Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Five species infect humans: P. falciparum (most deadly), P. vivax, P. malariae, P. ovale, and P. knowlesi.

## Transmission
- Bite of infected female Anopheles mosquito (primary route)
- Blood transfusion from infected donor
- Sharing needles
- Mother to child during pregnancy or childbirth (congenital malaria)

## Symptoms
Symptoms appear 10–15 days after mosquito bite:
- **Classic malaria triad**: Fever, chills, sweating (cyclical pattern every 48–72 hours)
- Headache
- Muscle aches and fatigue
- Nausea and vomiting
- Anemia (due to destruction of red blood cells)
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes)

**Severe Malaria (P. falciparum):**
- Cerebral malaria (seizures, coma)
- Severe anemia
- Respiratory distress
- Organ failure
- Hypoglycemia

## Diagnosis
- **Blood smear microscopy**: Gold standard; identifies species and parasite density
- **Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs)**: Detect malaria antigens; results in 15–20 minutes
- **PCR**: Most sensitive; used for species confirmation
- **Complete blood count**: Shows anemia, thrombocytopenia

## Treatment
- **P. falciparum (uncomplicated)**: Artemisinin-based Combination Therapies (ACTs) — artemether-lumefantrine, artesunate-amodiaquine
- **Severe malaria**: IV artesunate (preferred) or IV quinine
- **P. vivax/P. ovale**: Chloroquine + Primaquine (to eliminate liver stage)
- Treatment must be completed to prevent recurrence

## Prevention
- **Mosquito nets**: Long-lasting insecticidal nets (LLINs) — most effective
- **Indoor residual spraying (IRS)**: Insecticide sprayed on walls
- **Chemoprophylaxis**: Antimalarial drugs for travelers (atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, mefloquine)
- **Mosquito repellents**: DEET-containing products
- **Vaccine**: RTS,S/AS01 (Mosquirix) — first approved malaria vaccine; recommended for children in sub-Saharan Africa
- Wearing protective clothing (long sleeves, pants)
