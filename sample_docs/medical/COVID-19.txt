# COVID-19

## Overview
COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019 and declared a global pandemic by the WHO in March 2020.

## Transmission
- Primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and aerosols from infected persons
- Close contact (within 6 feet) with an infected person
- Touching contaminated surfaces then touching face (less common)
- Incubation period: 2–14 days (average 5–6 days)

## Symptoms
**Mild to Moderate:**
- Fever or chills
- Cough (usually dry)
- Fatigue
- Muscle or body aches
- Headache
- Loss of taste or smell (anosmia/ageusia)
- Sore throat
- Runny nose
- Nausea or vomiting
- Diarrhea

**Severe:**
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent chest pain or pressure
- Confusion or inability to stay awake
- Pale, gray, or blue-colored skin, lips, or nail beds

## Diagnosis
- RT-PCR (Reverse Transcription Polymerase Chain Reaction) — gold standard
- Rapid antigen tests
- Chest CT scan showing ground-glass opacities
- Blood tests (CBC, CRP, D-dimer, ferritin)

## Treatment
- **Mild cases**: Rest, hydration, fever reducers (acetaminophen/ibuprofen)
- **Antivirals**: Nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid), remdesivir for hospitalized patients
- **Severe cases**: Supplemental oxygen, corticosteroids (dexamethasone), mechanical ventilation
- **Monoclonal antibodies**: For high-risk patients

## Prevention
- Vaccination (mRNA vaccines: Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna; viral vector: Johnson & Johnson)
- Wearing masks in high-risk settings
- Hand hygiene (washing with soap for 20 seconds)
- Physical distancing
- Ventilation of indoor spaces
- Isolation when infected

## Long COVID
Some patients experience persistent symptoms for weeks or months after acute infection:
- Fatigue, brain fog, shortness of breath, joint pain, depression
- Affects approximately 10–30% of infected individuals
