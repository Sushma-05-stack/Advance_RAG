# Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

## Overview
Hypertension is a chronic condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high (≥130/80 mmHg). It is called the "silent killer" because it often has no symptoms but significantly increases risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

## Classification
- **Normal**: Systolic <120 mmHg AND Diastolic <80 mmHg
- **Elevated**: Systolic 120–129 AND Diastolic <80
- **Stage 1 Hypertension**: Systolic 130–139 OR Diastolic 80–89
- **Stage 2 Hypertension**: Systolic ≥140 OR Diastolic ≥90
- **Hypertensive Crisis**: Systolic >180 and/or Diastolic >120

## Causes
- **Primary (Essential) Hypertension**: No identifiable cause; accounts for 90–95% of cases; linked to genetics, age, obesity, high salt intake
- **Secondary Hypertension**: Caused by kidney disease, thyroid disorders, sleep apnea, certain medications

## Symptoms
Most people have no symptoms. When present:
- Headaches (especially in the morning)
- Dizziness
- Nosebleeds
- Shortness of breath
- Chest pain (in severe cases)

## Diagnosis
- Blood pressure measured on two or more separate occasions
- 24-hour ambulatory blood pressure monitoring
- Blood tests (kidney function, cholesterol, blood sugar)
- ECG to check heart

## Treatment
- **Lifestyle changes**: Reduce sodium intake (<2.3g/day), DASH diet, regular exercise, weight loss, limit alcohol, quit smoking
- **Medications**: ACE inhibitors (lisinopril), ARBs (losartan), calcium channel blockers (amlodipine), diuretics (hydrochlorothiazide), beta-blockers

## Complications
- Heart attack and heart failure
- Stroke
- Chronic kidney disease
- Vision loss (hypertensive retinopathy)
- Peripheral artery disease
- Aneurysm
