# Diabetes

## Overview
Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels (hyperglycemia) resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both. There are three main types: Type 1, Type 2, and Gestational diabetes.

## Types
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: An autoimmune condition where the immune system destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. Requires lifelong insulin therapy. Usually diagnosed in children and young adults.
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: The most common form (90-95% of cases). The body either doesn't produce enough insulin or doesn't use it effectively (insulin resistance). Strongly linked to obesity and sedentary lifestyle.
- **Gestational Diabetes**: Develops during pregnancy and usually resolves after delivery, but increases risk of Type 2 diabetes later.

## Symptoms
- Frequent urination (polyuria)
- Excessive thirst (polydipsia)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue and weakness
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds
- Frequent infections
- Tingling or numbness in hands/feet

## Diagnosis
- **Fasting Blood Glucose**: ≥126 mg/dL on two separate occasions
- **HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)**: ≥6.5% indicates diabetes; 5.7–6.4% indicates prediabetes
- **Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)**: Blood glucose ≥200 mg/dL two hours after consuming 75g glucose
- **Random Blood Glucose**: ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms

## Treatment
- **Type 1**: Daily insulin injections or insulin pump therapy; carbohydrate counting; continuous glucose monitoring
- **Type 2**: Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, weight loss); oral medications (Metformin is first-line); GLP-1 receptor agonists; SGLT2 inhibitors; insulin if needed
- **Diet**: Low glycemic index foods, reduced refined carbohydrates, increased fiber
- **Exercise**: At least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week

## Complications
- **Cardiovascular disease**: Leading cause of death in diabetics
- **Diabetic nephropathy**: Kidney damage leading to chronic kidney disease
- **Diabetic retinopathy**: Damage to blood vessels in the retina, leading to blindness
- **Diabetic neuropathy**: Nerve damage causing pain, numbness, and digestive issues
- **Diabetic foot**: Poor circulation and neuropathy leading to ulcers and amputation risk

## Prevention
Type 2 diabetes can be prevented or delayed through:
- Maintaining healthy body weight (BMI 18.5–24.9)
- Regular physical activity
- Healthy diet rich in vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
- Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol
