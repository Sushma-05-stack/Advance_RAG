# HIV/AIDS

## Overview
HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the immune system, specifically CD4+ T cells (helper T cells). If untreated, HIV can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome), the most advanced stage of HIV infection.

## Transmission
- Unprotected sexual contact (vaginal, anal, oral)
- Sharing needles, syringes, or drug equipment
- Blood transfusions with infected blood
- Mother to child during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding
- NOT transmitted by: casual contact, hugging, sharing food, mosquito bites

## Stages
1. **Acute HIV Infection**: 2–4 weeks after exposure; flu-like symptoms; very high viral load; highly contagious
2. **Chronic HIV Infection (Clinical Latency)**: Virus is active but reproduces slowly; may last 10+ years; few or no symptoms
3. **AIDS**: CD4 count <200 cells/mm³ or presence of AIDS-defining illness; severe immune deficiency

## Symptoms
**Acute Stage:**
- Fever, chills, rash
- Night sweats
- Muscle aches, sore throat
- Fatigue, swollen lymph nodes
- Mouth ulcers

**AIDS Stage:**
- Rapid weight loss
- Recurring fever
- Extreme fatigue
- Prolonged swelling of lymph glands
- Opportunistic infections (pneumocystis pneumonia, toxoplasmosis, CMV)
- Certain cancers (Kaposi's sarcoma, lymphoma)

## Diagnosis
- **HIV Antibody/Antigen Test**: Detects HIV antibodies and p24 antigen; most common screening test
- **HIV RNA (Viral Load) Test**: Detects virus directly; used for early diagnosis and monitoring
- **CD4 Count**: Measures immune system health; normal is 500–1500 cells/mm³
- **Western Blot**: Confirmatory test

## Treatment
- **Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)**: Combination of HIV medicines taken daily; suppresses viral load to undetectable levels
- **Common regimens**: Integrase inhibitors (dolutegravir) + 2 NRTIs (tenofovir + emtricitabine)
- **Goal**: Undetectable viral load = Untransmittable (U=U)
- ART does not cure HIV but allows people to live long, healthy lives

## Prevention
- **PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)**: Daily medication (tenofovir/emtricitabine) for HIV-negative high-risk individuals; 99% effective
- **PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)**: Emergency medication taken within 72 hours of potential exposure
- Consistent condom use
- Not sharing needles
- Regular HIV testing
- Treating HIV-positive pregnant women to prevent mother-to-child transmission
