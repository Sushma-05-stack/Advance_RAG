# Heart Failure

## Overview
Heart failure (HF) is a chronic condition where the heart muscle doesn't pump blood as well as it should. It does not mean the heart has stopped — it means the heart is not pumping efficiently enough to meet the body's needs.

## Types
- **HFrEF (Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction)**: EF <40%; systolic dysfunction; heart muscle is weak
- **HFpEF (Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction)**: EF ≥50%; diastolic dysfunction; heart muscle is stiff
- **HFmrEF**: EF 41–49%; mildly reduced
- **Left-sided HF**: Fluid backs up into lungs (pulmonary congestion)
- **Right-sided HF**: Fluid backs up into body (peripheral edema)
- **Biventricular HF**: Both sides affected

## Causes
- Coronary artery disease and heart attack (most common)
- Hypertension
- Cardiomyopathy (dilated, hypertrophic, restrictive)
- Valvular heart disease
- Arrhythmias (atrial fibrillation)
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Alcohol and drug abuse
- Chemotherapy

## Symptoms
- Shortness of breath (dyspnea) — especially with exertion or lying flat (orthopnea)
- Waking up breathless at night (paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea)
- Persistent cough or wheezing (pink, foamy mucus)
- Swelling (edema) in legs, ankles, feet
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat
- Fatigue and weakness
- Reduced ability to exercise
- Nausea, lack of appetite
- Difficulty concentrating

## NYHA Classification
- **Class I**: No symptoms with ordinary activity
- **Class II**: Slight limitation; comfortable at rest
- **Class III**: Marked limitation; comfortable only at rest
- **Class IV**: Symptoms at rest; unable to carry on any activity

## Diagnosis
- **Echocardiogram**: Measures ejection fraction; most important test
- **BNP/NT-proBNP**: Elevated in heart failure; used for diagnosis and monitoring
- **Chest X-ray**: Shows cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion
- **ECG**: Identifies arrhythmias, prior MI
- **Blood tests**: CBC, metabolic panel, thyroid function

## Treatment
**HFrEF:**
- ACE inhibitors or ARBs (reduce afterload)
- Beta-blockers (carvedilol, metoprolol succinate)
- Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (spironolactone)
- SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin) — new standard of care
- Diuretics (furosemide) for fluid management
- ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan) — superior to ACE inhibitors

**Devices:**
- ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator) for sudden cardiac death prevention
- CRT (Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy) for dyssynchrony

**Advanced HF:**
- LVAD (Left Ventricular Assist Device)
- Heart transplantation

## Lifestyle Management
- Fluid restriction (1.5–2 L/day)
- Low-sodium diet (<2g/day)
- Daily weight monitoring
- Regular moderate exercise (cardiac rehabilitation)
- Avoid alcohol and smoking
